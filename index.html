Welcome on  Data-driven Workgroup for Applied Research Fields (DWARF)


From Earth systems to medical imaging

Description

The primarily aim of the group is to predict from real world data by using advanced computational techniques but also playing and testing new approaches. It is a highly multi-disciplinary group, embracing applied mathematics, computer science and earth systems. Mainly we operate on the ground of inverse problems or so-called data assimilation, in the area of geosciences (oceanography, subsurface energy system, coastal environment) and medical imaging (brain cancer). Our group is real world data driven with an important sake for generalisation of the developed method.

Spontaneous candidates are very appreciated, do not hesitate to contact us for an internship (master level), PhD study or Postdoc.

The Team

Claus ARANHA

I am a Researcher and Educator at the University of Tsukuba in Japan. My main research fields are Evolutionary Computation and Artificial Life. In the DWARF these topics can be applied as Simulation (artificial life) and Optimization (evolutionary computation) for Operation Research and Engineering problems. Short term, my research goals are the development of complex simulation and efficient and self adaptive optimization algorithms. Long term, my research goals are the development and study of artificial life forms based on self-replicating programs.

Romain CHASSAGNE

Researcher and teacher at Heriot-Watt University in UK, I work on earth systems monitoring and forecasting, at the cross roads between Geophysics, Maths and Computer science. I consider data is the center of any data assimilation projects and it has to be understood and handled properly. In other words pre-processing is also a part of my research, like the assimilation itself. I try to drive my research towards generalisation and automatisation of the methods and methodologies. In terms of themes, I work on transitional energy: geothermal, energy storage, CCS from a monitoring and forecasting perspective.

Julien DAMBRINE
